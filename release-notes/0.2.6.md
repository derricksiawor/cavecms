## 0.2.6

Restores finish cleanly on shared hosting, you can change your own password, and the dashboard wears the CaveCMS badge.

### Fixed — restores no longer undo themselves on cPanel

On cPanel/LiteSpeed a restore could put all your content back, reach the final "Verifying" step, then stall and roll itself back — even though the restore had actually succeeded. The check that confirms your site is healthy was knocking on a door the host doesn't expose. It now checks your site the way visitors actually reach it, so a good restore completes and stays. (The same fix also protects the automatic rollback after a failed update from a false "your site is offline" alarm.)

### New — change your own password

There's now an **Account** page in the admin where you can change your own password: enter your current one, choose a new one. Changing it signs you out everywhere else, on this device you stay logged in. Available to every signed-in user.

### Improved — a clean sign-in after a restore

A restore replaces your site's user accounts with the ones from the backup, so your old browser session no longer matches. After a restore you're now signed out and asked to log in fresh with a restored account — no more being stuck in a stale session that rejects your password or hides the admin bar.

### Improved — restore uploads warn before you leave

Uploading a backup file to restore from happens in your browser, so leaving the page mid-upload would quietly cancel it. The browser now asks you to confirm before navigating away while an upload is in progress.

### Improved — the admin wears the CaveCMS badge

The admin dashboard's browser-tab icon is now the CaveCMS mark, so it's easy to spot among your tabs — distinct from your public site's own favicon.
