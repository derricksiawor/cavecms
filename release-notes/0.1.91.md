## 0.1.91

A complete, WordPress-class blog — built the CaveCMS way. Your posts are now made of the same blocks as your pages, you get a real Blog page plus a posts section you can drop on any page, categories and tags with their own archive pages, scheduling, an RSS feed, related posts, and URLs you control. The whole thing follows your theme, so it looks right the moment you turn it on.

### New — posts are real CMS blocks now

A post body used to be plain text. Now it's a full **block tree** — the very same editor your pages use. Drop in headings, images, galleries, quotes, callouts, anything in the block library, and edit a post right on the live page with the inline drawer you already know. Your existing posts carry over automatically, so nothing is lost. Posts are first-class CaveCMS content at last.

### New — a posts section you can put on any page

A new **Posts** block with five polished layouts: **Grid**, **Cards**, **List**, **Magazine** (a big featured lead with a tidy grid beneath), and a swipeable **Carousel**. Put it on your homepage to show your latest writing, on a landing page filtered to a single category, or let it power your Blog page and archives automatically. Choose where the posts come from — latest, a category or tag, an author, a hand-picked set, or "related to the post you're reading" — then set the columns, card style, what each card shows, and how it loads more (numbered pages, a load-more button, or none). It's keyboard-accessible, doesn't shift the layout as images load, and adapts to your theme out of the box.

### New — categories, tags, and archive pages

Organise your writing with **categories** and **tags**, assigned from a visual picker (you see the terms, not a list of names). Each one gets its own clean archive page, is listed in your sitemap and understood by search engines, and your posts cross-link to them — so readers can follow a topic instead of hitting a dead end.

### New — schedule posts and manage them like a pro

Write a **draft**, set it to **publish on a future date**, and it goes live on its own — no plugin, no cron job, nothing to remember. The Posts screen is now a proper workspace: status tabs for **Draft / Scheduled / Published / Trash** with live counts, search, sortable columns, and **bulk actions** to publish, trash, or tag several posts at once.

### New — your Blog, your settings, your links

A new **Settings → Blog** screen controls how your blog reads — default layout, posts per page, what each card shows, feed length, and more. And a new **Settings → Permalinks** screen lets you change the base of your blog and projects URLs (for example **/blog → /journal** or **/projects → /work**). Old links keep working — they redirect to the new address automatically — so you never break a shared link or your search ranking.

### New — an RSS feed, related posts, and reading time

Readers can **subscribe** to your writing through a built-in RSS feed. Every post now shows an estimated **reading time** and a tasteful **"related posts"** rail at the end, and each page carries the right structured data so search engines and social cards understand it.

### Improved — your blog adapts to your theme

The entire blog — the index, the archives, the post pages, and the new posts section — now follows your selected theme automatically, so your text stays readable and on-brand whether your palette is light or dark.
