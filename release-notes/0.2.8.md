## 0.2.8

Stops a wrong Site URL from quietly breaking updates, and makes the updater say exactly what's wrong when it can't reach your site.

### Fixed — your Site URL can't accidentally be set to localhost

When you install CaveCMS you often open the setup page through a private address like `localhost`, before your real domain is pointed at the server. Setup used to pre-fill that private address as your Site URL, and accepting it left your site configured to talk to itself instead of the outside world. That quietly broke automatic updates, your sitemap, and the links in your emails. Setup now recognises a private address and asks you for your real public domain instead, and it won't save a localhost Site URL on a live site. The command-line installer does the same.

### Improved — updates tell you when they can't reach your site

If a site's Site URL is wrong, an update used to stop with a vague "your site isn't responding" and no clue why. Now it names the address it tried to reach and, when that address is localhost, tells you plainly to set your real domain under Settings, General, then try again. The same clear message appears for restores. And if your Site URL is already set to localhost, the Updates page shows a notice with a one-click link to fix it, so you find out before an update fails.
