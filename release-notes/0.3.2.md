## 0.3.2

Fixes a sign-in lockout after a single typo, and keeps your site's own web address when you restore a backup.

### Fixed — a wrong password no longer locks you out

Mistype your password once and every following attempt was rejected, even with the correct password, until you reloaded the page. The sign-in form's one-time security token wasn't refreshed after a failed attempt, so the next try arrived with a spent token and was turned away. The form now refreshes it automatically, so you can just correct your password and sign in. Your account's brute-force protection is unchanged.

### Improved — restores keep your site's own web address

When you restore a backup that was taken on a different domain (a migration, a clone, or recovering onto a new server), the restore used to bring back the old site's web address along with the content, which broke your email links, sitemap, and updates. A restore now re-points the Site URL to the domain this install actually serves, automatically, on every kind of hosting (cPanel, nginx, Apache, or a local install). Restoring a full backup that includes its server settings still keeps the backup's address, as intended.
