## 0.1.90

A big one. Your whole site can now be run from a terminal or an AI assistant — finer-grained tokens, a built-in MCP server, and sync, backups, and publishing all reachable without signing in. Plus animated slideshow sections, a built-in cookie-consent banner, automatic contrast, and a round of SEO polish and fixes.

### New — a built-in MCP server: hand your site to an AI assistant

In 0.1.84 we added an HTTP API with tokens so AI tools could edit your site by prompt. CaveCMS now also speaks **MCP** — the Model Context Protocol that assistants like Claude and Cursor use to call tools directly. Point your assistant at your site with a token and it can work through native CaveCMS tools: read your live theme, describe the block library, create and edit pages, posts and projects, manage media and navigation, and more — the same guardrails as the API, but a far smoother experience for the assistant. The goal is simple: one place — your terminal or your AI tool — that can do almost everything.

### New — finer, safer tokens (per-resource scopes + a read-only role)

Tokens used to be **editor** or **admin**, with the run of all your content and branding. Now you can scope a token to exactly what a tool needs:

- **Per-resource scopes.** Grant a token access to just pages, posts, projects, blocks, media, navigation, settings, sync, or backups — and choose **read-only** or **read-and-write** for each. Hand an agent a token that can edit pages but never touch settings, and it simply cannot.
- **A new viewer role.** For tokens that should only ever look, never change — perfect for read-only integrations, dashboards, or a cautious first run with a new tool.
- **Everything from before still holds.** Tokens remain shown once, revocable in one click, optionally expiring — and still can't reach your user accounts, secrets, or the updater.

### New — run sync, backups, and publishing without signing in

The features that already live in your dashboard are now reachable headlessly, so you can script them or let an AI assistant drive them:

- **Move a site from local to production.** Push a whole local or staging copy of your site up to your live install — or pull production back down to work on it safely — over the API or MCP, using named production targets you set up once. The transfer stages first and switches over cleanly, so your live site is never caught half-changed.
- **Back up and restore from the terminal.** Trigger a backup, set destinations, schedule, or encryption, or restore a previous one — all without opening the dashboard, each gated by the token's scope.
- **Edit a draft, then publish.** An assistant can now make its changes to your private **draft** and then **publish**, **undo**, **redo**, or **discard** — the same draft-to-publish flow your editor uses. Automated edits stay invisible to visitors until they're published, so nothing an agent does goes live by accident.

### New — animated slideshow section backgrounds

A section or column background can now be a **slideshow** instead of a single image. Add several photos and they cross-fade on a timer you set, with an optional slow **Ken Burns** zoom for a cinematic, premium feel. Headings and buttons layered on top stay crisp and legible throughout. Ideal for heroes, full-bleed bands, and cover sections — set it from the section's background options, no code required.

### New — a built-in cookie-consent banner

CaveCMS now ships its own **cookie-consent banner**, ready for GDPR. Under **Settings → Cookies**, switch it on and write your own categories and descriptions in plain language. Visitors get a clear choice over analytics and marketing cookies, and that choice is wired straight into Google's consent mode — your analytics and ad tags stay switched off until a visitor agrees, while strictly-necessary cookies always work. No plugin, no third-party banner, nothing to buy.

### Improved — text stays readable on any background, automatically

Recolour a section or reach for a bold palette and CaveCMS now anchors your text colour to stay high-contrast against whatever sits behind it — no more wondering whether light text will wash out on a pale band or dark text vanish on a deep one. It also understands short (3-character), standard (6-character), and with-transparency (8-character) hex colours everywhere you can type one.

### Improved — share previews for every platform

The **Social & Schema** screen used to preview only how a link looks on X. It now leads with a live **Open Graph** preview — the share card almost every app renders, from Facebook, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp to Slack, iMessage, and Discord — with the X-specific card alongside it. You can see exactly how a shared link will look, built from your real title, description, and share image, before you publish.

### Improved — clearer SEO details

- **Real search-engine logos** throughout Connect & Verify and the indexing settings — Google, Bing, Yandex, Baidu, Naver, Pinterest and the rest now show their actual marks instead of plain initials.
- **The sitemap's "what to include" options** are laid out one per line, so they're easy to scan and toggle.

### Fixed — your sitemap no longer lists a page twice

Your blog landing page could appear twice in the sitemap. It's now listed once, keeping a clean, unambiguous map for search engines.

### Fixed — turning AI off always saves

Unticking **Enable AI features** now switches off every AI surface along with it. Before, the surface toggles stayed on behind the scenes, so simply turning AI off could fail to save with a vague "some fields look off." Disabling AI — and clearing your saved key — now saves cleanly, every time.

### Fixed — release notes read properly

The **What's new** notes on the Updates screen and the dashboard's update card now render as formatted text — headings, emphasis, and lists — instead of showing raw markdown.

Updating applies everything automatically.
