## 0.1.87

Edit freely without going live: every change now saves to a private **draft** you **publish** when you're ready — with **undo and redo** that survive a refresh. Plus, keep an **off-site copy of your whole site** in your own Google Drive or OneDrive, with automatic backups and one-click restore.

### New — your edits save to a draft, and go live when you publish

Until now, every edit changed your live site the moment you made it. Now your changes save to a private **draft** instead, and your published site stays exactly as it is until you decide. Tweak a headline, recolour a section, rearrange a column — leave and come back tomorrow to keep going — and none of it is public yet. The admin bar shows how many **unpublished changes** you have, with **Publish** and **Discard** right there: **Publish** makes everything live at once, and **Discard** throws the draft away and restores the published version. There's nothing to set up — editing simply works this way now, and your visitors only ever see what you've published.

### New — undo and redo that stick

**Undo** and **redo** now work across your whole page. Press **⌘Z** (or the Undo arrow in the admin bar) to step back through your recent changes — edits, reorders, inserts, deletes — and **⇧⌘Z** to step forward again. Because your history is saved on the server, it survives a refresh: close the tab, come back later, and you can still undo right where you left off.

### New — back up to your own Google Drive or OneDrive

Backups have always been written to your server, which protects you from a bad edit but not from losing the server itself. Now, under **Settings → Backups**, you can connect your own **Google Drive** or **OneDrive** and keep an off-site copy somewhere a server failure can't reach.

Connecting takes a few seconds and never asks you to copy-and-paste keys or secrets: pick a provider, a short code appears, you approve it on Google or Microsoft's own page, and you're connected. Your account email shows next to the provider so you always know where backups land, and you can disconnect any time.

Once connected:

- **Send backups to** the cloud, this server, or both — "Back up now" and your scheduled backups follow the choice.
- **Encrypt with a passphrase.** Turn it on and the cloud copy is locked with a passphrase only you know, so even your cloud provider can't read your site's contents. There's a one-click generator if you'd like a strong one. Keep it safe — it's the only key to an encrypted backup if your server is ever gone.
- **Keep this many in the cloud.** Choose how many backups to retain off-site; older ones are tidied up automatically after each new one.

### New — automatic backups on a schedule

Set backups to run **daily** or **weekly** and CaveCMS makes them for you in the background — no admin tab has to be open. Paired with a cloud destination, your site quietly keeps an up-to-date off-site copy on its own.

### New — restore from the cloud

If a server is ever lost, **Restore from the cloud** pulls a backup straight back from your connected account. A safety snapshot is taken first and the restore rolls back automatically if anything goes wrong, so a failed restore never leaves your site half-changed. Encrypted backups restore seamlessly using the passphrase you set.

And when a restore genuinely can't proceed, it now tells you **exactly why**: a wrong passphrase says so — "check that the passphrase matches the one used when the backup was made" — instead of blaming your connection, and a corrupted or altered download is caught and refused before anything is touched.

Everything here is included — there's nothing to buy.

### Also fixed

- **First-time sign-in for new team members.** When you add someone under **Settings → Users**, they're now guided to choose their own password the first time they sign in — then dropped straight into the dashboard, signed in and ready. Their temporary password can only be used once, and they're asked to pick something at least 12 characters that isn't the temporary one.

Updating applies everything automatically.
